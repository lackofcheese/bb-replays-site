FUMBBL match 4719385 · Sunny Coast Slam practice · 2026-07-18

Jawa brought knives. Dimitri brought a net.

Mahaha 0 – 2 Slam Union

Mahaha conceded, H2T3. Sunny Coast Slam practice, with no lasting consequences.

Very Sunny. Two Assassins. Two Witch Elves. Five stabs. Zero permanent injuries. Somewhere in the glare, Mahaha's murder mystery became a workplace comedy.

Jawa won the toss and kicked. That was not the safe Dark Elf choice. Receiving would have meant first blocks, first blood and a chance to make eleven Union elves feel like nine before halftime. Jawa wanted the more ambitious prize: attack Dimitri's offence at full strength, steal the ball or squeeze out an early score, then receive after the break. The plan had teeth. For six turns, it kept finding the carrier.

Dimitri answered with the least Elven Union opening imaginable. No passes. No hurry. The sun had put a tax on throwing, so the Leader Thrower picked up and Slam Union began an eight-turn trudge through a cloud of perfume, sharpened steel and terrible intentions.

The first removal was almost the player who would decide the match. Slam Union's Frenzy Blitzer followed one block too far, ate the Dark Elf Wrestle lineman's reply and went to the KO box. Dimitri sent on the apothecary immediately. The Blitzer stayed on the pitch stunned, which looked like an expensive way to preserve a headache. By the end of the game he had made the winning sack and collected the MVP. Best fifty thousand Dimitri spent all night.

Mahaha did not merely rattle the cage. On turn three, Jawa ran a lineman through the missing corner and rolled one die at the ball. Push. Reroll. Push. The Union Wrestle lineman, flat on his back and apparently decorative, occupied the only square that could have supplied a second die. One prone elf, one square, one drive still alive.

Then the Union hit back. The Diving Tackle Blitzer badly hurt a Block Witch Elf and forced Jawa's apothecary to return her to reserves. The Leader Thrower escaped west on two rushes. A Catcher took over near the sideline, where a Witch Elf came within one difficult dodge of surfing player, ball and all. Another Catcher carried east behind one lonely screen, and Jawa reached him too. The first half was not a grind. It was a parcel being passed across a knife fight.

Five block dice reached Union carriers across the half. None found a knockdown. That is cruel, but it is not the same as harmless. Mahaha kept opening the door; the ball simply refused to walk through it. Slam Union rebuilt the box on turn seven, handed off on turn eight and crossed for 1-0. Catch saved the final hand-off after the first roll came up 1. Even the touchdown wanted a little drama.

At halftime the assassinations ledger read five stabs, two stuns and no removals. The scoreboard was less kind.

Then came Charge!

Five open Union players moved before Mahaha had an activation. The free blitz managed only a push, but it pinned an Assassin and poured Union shirts across the line. The kickoff did not steal the ball. It stole the clean patch of grass where Jawa meant to build the drive.

Mahaha's first turn was their best: three armour breaks and a clean pickup by the Leader Runner. It still ended face-down. An Assassin made his dodge, the Diving Tackle Blitzer objected, and a successful escape became a fall and a stun. On the reply, the Frenzy Blitzer lined up a sideline surf and rolled both-down against a lineman without Block. No crowd required. The lineman was knocked out on the spot, the only removal of the night that stayed removed.

Jawa built a proper four-corner cage. Dimitri did not charge it. He wrapped nine players around it and waited. The Diving Tackle Blitzer sat on both prone Assassins. Every useful advance began in a Union tacklezone. Mahaha could stay put and be strangled, or break for open grass and accept the dice. Jawa chose the break, and he was right to try it.

The order was the killer. One Blitzer crossed the screen and left a cage corner. Then a Witch Elf left the other western corner and rushed forward before the Leader Runner had moved. She rolled 1. Leader reroll. Another 1. Armour broken. Turn over. The breakout froze halfway through, with its runners ahead of the ball and both doors hanging open.

The preserved Frenzy Blitzer came through the gap on two Dodge-covered steps. The Leader Runner had Dump-Off and one legal ambition: throw through three tacklezones in Very Sunny weather. Seven-plus on a d6 meant natural 6 or bust. With a sack already arriving and the ball likely to spill, taking the one-in-six escape hatch was worth it.

No six.

The pass fumbled. Both-down flattened the Runner. A Catcher picked up the loose ball, rushed and handed it to the other Catcher. Nerves of Steel held. Dodge held. One last rush held. Five consecutive 2+ rolls turned a defensive net into a touchdown in eleven seconds.

Two-nil. Five turns remained. Jawa conceded, losing nothing but a practice result and gaining one useful conclusion: for the actual Sunny Coast Slam, Mahaha are out and the Slann are in.

The knives were bad enough. Next time Dimitri gets frogs.
