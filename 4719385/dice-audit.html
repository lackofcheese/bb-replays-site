Dice commentary

The headline numbers were close to fair. Skull rates were near-identical, block-related armour breaks landed close to expectation for both sides, and dodge results were comparable. There's even a perfectly symmetric wound: each coach lost his Leader reroll to double 1s on a rush.

But Jawa has a legitimate grievance about where the variance landed. He got dice on the ball three separate times in the first half, five one-die rolls in total, and never saw the POW; any one of them landing turns the T5-T6 wobble into a crisis, because the Union carrier protection was genuinely breachable in that stretch. Meanwhile Dimitri's snake eyes (the T3 rush, the T6 dodge) arrived on final actions of turns that were already banked. So the honest version is: Dimitri consistently structured turns so that failure was cheap, which is skill, and his failures also happened to be the cheap ones while Jawa's came at maximum leverage, which is fortune.

As for the decisive moment: the second-half break-out was a defensible and probably necessary gamble for a coach a player down with Diving Tackle pinning his Assassins and a press closing in. The sequencing analysis in the interactive analysis is the real critique: ball first, screens second, optional rushes last or never. The dice punished the inversion as hard as dice can, and that, plus a first half in which five one-die rolls at the ball produced nothing, is a rough way to lose. The scoreline reads harsher than the play was.
